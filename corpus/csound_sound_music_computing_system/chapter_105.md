3. A short rhythm is given in proportions, for instance 1/2, 2/3, 1/3, 1/4, 3/4, 1.
This rhythm controls the playback of isolated sound units, so that the natural,
free rhythm of the language is left in favour of a metric rhythm. To avoid perfect
repetitions, the rhythm is varied by applying permutations and different scalings
of expansion/compression.
